# 📖 Project Documentation

# 📊 Business Performance Analytics using Microsoft Excel

---

# Introduction

Businesses generate large volumes of sales and financial data every day. However, raw data alone provides limited value unless it is transformed into meaningful insights that support business decision-making.

This project demonstrates how Microsoft Excel can be utilized as a Business Intelligence tool to transform raw sales and financial data into interactive dashboards and analytical reports. By leveraging Power Query, Power Pivot, DAX, and Pivot Tables, the project provides a comprehensive view of business performance across customers, markets, and fiscal periods.

---

# Business Problem

The organization operates across multiple customers, products, and international markets, generating a significant volume of sales and financial data. While the data was available, reporting relied heavily on manual processes, making it difficult to analyze business performance efficiently.

Key business challenges included:

* Limited visibility into customer performance.
* Difficulty comparing market performance against sales targets.
* Time-consuming financial reporting.
* Lack of centralized KPI monitoring.
* Limited insight into profitability across markets and fiscal periods.

To address these challenges, an interactive Excel-based reporting solution was developed to automate reporting and provide meaningful business insights.

---

# Project Objective

The primary objective of this project was to build an interactive Business Intelligence solution that enables users to:

* Monitor customer sales performance.
* Compare market performance against business targets.
* Analyze Profit & Loss across multiple fiscal periods.
* Track revenue growth and profitability.
* Develop financial KPIs using DAX.
* Support strategic business decision-making through interactive reports.

---

# Project Workflow

The project was completed through the following stages.

## Step 1 – Data Collection

Business data was collected from multiple worksheets containing:

* Sales Transactions
* Customer Information
* Product Details
* Market Information
* Financial Records
* Date Dimension

---

## Step 2 – Data Cleaning & Transformation

Power Query was used to prepare the data by:

* Removing duplicate records
* Handling missing values
* Standardizing data formats
* Merging multiple datasets
* Transforming raw data into analysis-ready tables

This ensured consistency, improved data quality, and reduced manual effort during reporting.

---

## Step 3 – Data Modeling

A relational data model was developed using Power Pivot.

Relationships were established between:

* Customer Table
* Product Table
* Market Table
* Sales Table
* Date Table

The data model enabled dynamic filtering and seamless analysis across multiple reports.

---

## Step 4 – KPI Development

Business KPIs were developed using DAX measures, including:

* Total Net Sales
* Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
* Gross Margin
* Gross Margin %
* Target Achievement %
* Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth
* Sales Variance
* Revenue Contribution

These KPIs provide meaningful insights into both financial and operational performance.

# Dashboard Reports

## 1. Customer Performance Report

### Purpose

To evaluate customer-wise sales performance across multiple fiscal years.

### Report Features

* Customer-wise Net Sales
* Revenue Contribution
* Year-over-Year Growth
* Dynamic Filters
* Customer Comparison

### Business Value

This report enables management to identify high-value customers, monitor revenue growth, and evaluate customer contribution to overall business performance.

---

## 2. Market Performance vs Target

### Purpose

To compare actual sales performance with predefined business targets across different markets.

### Report Features

* Market-wise Sales Analysis
* Target Achievement
* Sales Variance
* Country-wise Performance Comparison

### Business Value

The report helps identify markets performing below expectations, enabling timely strategic decisions and performance improvement initiatives.

---

## 3. Profit & Loss Statement by Fiscal Year

### Purpose

To evaluate annual business profitability.

### KPIs Included

* Cost of Goods Sold

Provides management with a consolidated financial overview, enabling long-term performance evaluation and profitability analysis.

## 4. Profit & Loss Statement by Month
